## Featured Books

- [How to Win Friends and Influence People](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/how-to-win-friends-and-influence-people)
  - Author: Dale Carnegie
  - Shelf: Communication
  - Cadence: daily
  - Why it matters: Carnegie's book is a plainspoken manual for dealing with people by starting from their incentives, pride, fears, interests, and need to feel important. It argues that persuasion rarely begins with being right. It begins with lowering defensiveness, showing real interest, understanding the other person's point of view, giving sincere appreciation, and letting people keep dignity while changing their mind.
  - Source: https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/How-to-Win-Friends-and-Influence-People/Dale-Carnegie/9780671027032

- [Educated](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/educated)
  - Author: Tara Westover
  - Shelf: Memoir
  - Cadence: weekly
  - Why it matters: Educated is a memoir about growing up in an isolated survivalist family in Idaho, then slowly building a self through education, distance, memory, and the painful act of naming what happened. The book is not only about school. It is about how reality gets controlled inside families, how loyalty can fight perception, and how learning can create a self that the old world no longer recognizes.
  - Source: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/550168/educated-by-tara-westover/

- [Exhalation](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/exhalation)
  - Author: Ted Chiang
  - Shelf: Speculative Fiction
  - Cadence: weekly
  - Why it matters: Exhalation is a collection of speculative stories about time, choice, entropy, memory, artificial life, language, grief, and responsibility. Chiang's stories usually begin with a clean premise, then patiently follow its philosophical and emotional consequences. The result is fiction that feels like a thought experiment with a human cost.
  - Source: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/562270/exhalation-by-ted-chiang/

- [The Three-Body Problem](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/the-three-body-problem)
  - Author: Cixin Liu
  - Shelf: Science Fiction
  - Cadence: weekly
  - Why it matters: The Three-Body Problem starts in the trauma of the Cultural Revolution and expands into first contact, scientific disillusionment, planetary instability, game-like simulation, and civilizational strategy. The novel is less about aliens as monsters and more about what happens when people lose faith in humanity, discover a larger cosmic game, and make choices under extreme uncertainty.
  - Source: https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780765382030/thethreebodyproblem/

- [Blindsight + Echopraxia](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/blindsight-and-echopraxia)
  - Author: Peter Watts
  - Shelf: Science Fiction
  - Cadence: evergreen
  - Why it matters: Peter Watts's Firefall books are hard science fiction about alien contact, consciousness, intelligence, biology, and the uncomfortable possibility that awareness is not the same thing as usefulness. Blindsight in particular asks whether a mind can be intelligent without being conscious, and whether humans might be worse at understanding reality because we narrate ourselves too much.
  - Source: https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250237480/firefall/

- [Principles](https://books-radar.vercel.app/books/principles)
  - Author: Ray Dalio
  - Shelf: Personal Ops
  - Cadence: evergreen
  - Why it matters: Principles is Dalio's attempt to turn his life, investing, management, mistakes, and decision rules into an explicit operating system. The first half is autobiographical: curiosity, independence, painful mistakes, meditation, markets, and the habit of studying consequences. The second half turns into a rulebook for reality-facing, goal-setting, diagnosis, disagreement, and organizational decision-making.
  - Source: https://www.principles.com/principles/
